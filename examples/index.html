Example argument graphs for use with the Carneades Editor, an argument mapping and evaluation application.

These examples are all XML files in LKIF format. Download them to your computer and then open them locally using the Carneades application.

contract.xml
A simple but construed legal example, illustrating several features of the Carneades model of argument.
grevious-bodily-harm.xml
A example Bart Verheij used for his ArguMed system.
IBIS-Porsche.xml
A example IBIS diagram, first used in a paper on the Zeno Argumentation Framework.
PiersonPost.xml
A reconstruction of the arguments of the majority and dissenting opinions in the famous Pierson v Post court decision. The case is often used in property law courses and has often been used for experiments in the field of Artificial Intelligence and Law.
Pollock-red-light.xml
John Pollock's famous red light example, from his OSCAR system.
Prakken-Sartor-murder.xml
An example from Henry Prakken and Giovanni Sartor, for illustrating various kinds of burdens of proof.
SimonJones.xml
An example from the recent book "Elements of Argumentation" by Philippe Besnard and Anthony Hunter.
Toulmin.xml
Toulmin's famous example about whether or not Harry is a British subject.
Tweety.xml
And where AI be without the endless debate about whether or not Tweety can fly?
